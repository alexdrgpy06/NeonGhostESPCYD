## 2024-04-14 - PacketSniffer.cpp: Skipping Encrypted Data Frames
**Learning:** Found a significant bottleneck in `processPacket` when iterating over every single byte of an 802.11 data frame searching for the EAPOL ether type (0x888E). Encrypted payloads cannot possibly contain valid plaintext EAPOL headers anyway, making the iteration completely redundant for them. Checking the 'Protected' bit (`packet[1] & 0x40`) and ensuring a minimum length bound avoids scanning payload arrays of encrypted frames. This optimization yields a huge speedup (~24x in a mocked benchmark).
**Action:** Always verify header flags such as the 'Protected' bit before scanning data payloads for specific sequence signatures to bypass unnecessary looping. Include minimum bounds checking before array indexing.
